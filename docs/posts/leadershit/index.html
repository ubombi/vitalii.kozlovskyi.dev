The skill of Leadershit

January 21, 2023 | Suggest Changes

Disclaimer: I’m not an expert in Leadership or Behavioral Psychology.

Humans are social species, and tend to mimic others to reduce cognitive load. Having some measurement of success (E.G social status, work done) we select ourselves a “mentor”, and subconsciously repeating their behavior, hoping to achieve same results.

If the Monke realised a new way of getting a food, rest of the troop learns by following. We, Humans, do the same.

So, leadership is a role-model where the Leader shares superrior ideas with Followers, which is beneficial to the end goal. Leader is selected by Followers, based on their estimate of his success. Usually called inspiration.

Ignored Liability of the Leader#

Imagine reversed Only-Fans, where you pay for each follower. You can’t decide who follows you. Can’t force them. You pay with your time to understand them, to coach them, learn from them etc.

Followers, repeat your success, but also mistakes. Assuming, you are in a same boat, that multiplies your impact, but also liability. You pay with increased stress level, while the price of your mistake skyrockets.

Am I a Leader?#

Observe a group of potential followers for a while.

  • Do they subconsciously mimic you? Adopt your routine, style, habbits.
  • Do they ask for advice? Do they follow it?
  • Do they trust you, in general?
  • Are you sure, they don’t fear you?
  • Do you have a final word in trird-party disputes/arguments?
  • …

Authority doesn’t make you a Leader. People should follow you voluntary

if you are, it make sense to advance your career/business further.

Leadershit#

Trying to distinguish a mental ilnes from actual Leadership, I came up with simple word, with the following definition:

Leadershit is a combination of harmful skills with extremely bloated ego. These imposors like to discuss and improve leadershit. Enjoy calling each others leaders and maniacally add “leader” to any calendar event, job title, discussion, to prove their competence and be even more proud of themselves.

Yet, none of the pseudo-leaders can define leadership for you, nor provide an example of leader behavior.
